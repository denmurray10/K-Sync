# K-Beats Brand Guidelines

This document is the visual source of truth for K-Beats moving forward, based specifically on the current homepage direction. New pages and redesigns should inherit the homepage's color palette, font pairing, contrast style, and overall visual energy unless there is a strong product reason to diverge.

## Homepage Foundation

The homepage is the primary brand reference.

That means future design work should inherit:

- the black-stage background treatment
- the neon pink and cyan accent system
- the `Cinzel` plus `Montserrat` typography pairing
- the sharp-edged geometry
- the premium live-broadcast atmosphere
- the contrast-heavy, poster-like hero composition

If a new page feels softer, rounder, lighter, or more generic than the homepage, it is moving away from the brand.

## Brand Core

K-Beats should feel like a premium late-night K-pop broadcast platform: energetic, polished, fan-driven, and slightly theatrical without becoming noisy. The experience should combine music-channel drama with clean digital product clarity.

Brand keywords:

- electric
- editorial
- immersive
- confident
- fan-first
- modern nightlife

Avoid:

- soft pastel aesthetics
- generic SaaS dashboards
- rounded, bubbly components
- cluttered neon overload
- casual typography with weak hierarchy

## Visual Direction

The brand language is built on sharp edges, dark surfaces, bright signal colors, and dramatic contrast. It should feel more like a stage set or premium music broadcast than a generic entertainment blog.

Core principles:

- use black or near-black as the main stage
- use neon accents sparingly for emphasis, not as wallpaper
- keep silhouettes sharp and graphic
- let typography and spacing create authority before adding effects
- preserve a sense of motion and live energy

## Color System

These colors come directly from the homepage and should remain the primary palette.

### Homepage Core Palette

- `Primary Pink`: `#f425c0`
  This is the brand's main energy color. Use it for primary CTAs, active states, signature glows, key borders, and moments that need immediate recognition.
- `Electric Cyan`: `#00f0ff`
  This is the supporting contrast color. Use it for hover shifts, secondary accents, live-tech details, and cooler counterpoints to pink.
- `Black Stage`: `#000000`
  This is the dominant canvas color. Use it as the default page stage, hero base, and primary backdrop for the brand.
- `Soft Light Background`: `#f8f5f8`
  This exists in the homepage config but should be used sparingly. It is not the dominant brand mood.

### Supporting Neutrals

- `White`: `#ffffff`
  Use for primary text and high-contrast logo treatment.
- `Slate 200-500 family`
  Use for secondary labels, support text, metadata, and low-priority chrome.

### Usage Rules

- pink is the default action and identity color
- cyan is the default contrast accent
- black is the default background
- white is the default high-contrast text color
- do not introduce extra accent colors unless tied to a specific feature system
- do not replace the homepage palette with softer alternatives
- gradients are allowed only when they feel like brand energy, not decoration
- keep accent density controlled so pink and cyan still feel special

## Typography

The homepage establishes the approved font pairing:

- `Cinzel` for headings and ceremonial brand moments
- `Montserrat` for body copy, interface text, and utility content

### Type Roles

- Display headlines: `Cinzel`
- Section headings: `Cinzel`
- Premium navigation labels: `Cinzel`
- Body copy: `Montserrat`
- Forms, controls, metadata, search, and utility labels: `Montserrat`

### Type Behavior

- `Cinzel` should create drama, elegance, and ceremony
- `Montserrat` should keep the interface grounded and readable
- headings should feel stately and dramatic, not playful
- body text should stay clean and readable, not ornamental
- uppercase is appropriate for nav, labels, CTA text, and signal language like "Live On Air"
- wide tracking works for short labels and navigation, not for long paragraphs
- avoid substituting with unrelated fonts unless the brand guide is intentionally revised

### Hierarchy Rules

- H1 should feel unmistakably dominant on every page
- H2s should introduce clear editorial sections, not decorative subheads
- body copy should remain concise and support scanning
- support text should not compete with headlines through size or brightness

## Logo And Wordmark Behavior

The homepage/header wordmark treatment uses:

- white stroked `K`
- white hyphen
- pink stroked `beats`

This treatment should remain sharp, angular, and high-contrast.

Rules:

- keep the logo on calm, dark backgrounds whenever possible
- preserve enough negative space around the mark
- avoid placing the logo over noisy imagery
- do not soften the logo with rounded containers or pastel treatments

## Shape Language

K-Beats should use mostly square or near-square geometry.

Rules:

- default corners should be sharp
- pills are allowed only for intentional badges, status chips, or audio/live indicators
- avoid soft card radii across the product
- use borders and spacing more than soft shadows for separation

## Layout Principles

### Composition

- each section should have one primary visual job
- the first viewport must clearly communicate brand, offer, and action in the same bold way the homepage does
- layouts should feel poster-like at the top and more structured below
- avoid stacking many equal-weight cards when stronger editorial layout would work better

### Containers

- use a limited set of content widths
- align section edges consistently
- preserve stronger breathing room around hero content than around utility modules

### Density

- content-heavy areas can be dense, but they should still scan cleanly
- do not let fan content, charts, or news blocks become visually muddy
- when in doubt, remove weak elements before shrinking spacing

## Spacing System

Use a consistent spacing rhythm rather than one-off values.

Recommended spacing scale:

- `4px`
- `8px`
- `12px`
- `16px`
- `24px`
- `32px`
- `48px`
- `64px`
- `96px`

Rules:

- use `16px-24px` for tight internal component spacing
- use `32px-48px` for standard section spacing inside dense layouts
- use `64px-96px` for major section separation and hero breathing room
- mobile spacing should stay generous enough to preserve premium feel
- avoid collapsing vertical spacing just to fit more content above the fold

## Components

### Buttons

- primary buttons should use pink by default
- button labels should feel decisive and short
- corners should stay square or nearly square
- hover states can invert toward cyan or dark-on-pink
- strong CTA buttons should feel like broadcast controls, not app-store defaults

### Navigation

- desktop nav should feel ceremonial and precise
- active state should rely on color plus underline or emphasis, not color alone
- mobile menu should feel like a control deck, not a generic drawer

### Panels And Cards

- use panels only when grouping is necessary
- prefer translucent dark panels or dark solid blocks over white cards
- avoid card grids as the default answer for homepage sections

### Forms And Search

- use dark inputs with crisp borders
- prioritize clarity and contrast over decorative effects
- placeholder text should stay muted but still readable

## Imagery

Imagery should support fandom energy and music culture, not act as filler.

Rules:

- use artist photography, broadcast-inspired imagery, stage lighting, and performance atmosphere
- favor crops with a clear focal subject and calm zones for text
- avoid cluttered backgrounds with competing signage or text
- do not rely on generic gradients where artist or culture imagery would be stronger
- if imagery is unavailable, use strong graphic composition rather than fake lifestyle visuals

## Motion

The homepage establishes the motion tone: glowing accents, ticker movement, reveal transitions, and controlled hero animation.

Motion should reinforce live energy and premium polish.

Good uses:

- subtle hero movement
- ticker behavior
- hover glow or sheen
- reveal transitions that support hierarchy
- audio-reactive or broadcast-inspired accents

Rules:

- motion should feel smooth, not hyperactive
- keep loops restrained and purposeful
- always respect reduced-motion preferences
- if animation adds noise without meaning, remove it

## Responsive Rules

Every new page and major edit must be reviewed at:

- `1440px`
- `1024px`
- `768px`
- `390px`

Check for:

- no horizontal overflow
- no clipped headings
- no broken menu or player behavior
- intentional text wrapping
- comfortable tap targets
- stable hero composition

Mobile should not be treated as a compressed desktop layout. Re-stack, simplify, and reduce competing elements where needed.

## SEO And Content Standards

Brand consistency includes discoverability.

Rules:

- every page needs one clear topic and one clear H1
- title and description should sound like K-Beats, but remain search-readable
- avoid vague promotional copy that hides what the page actually offers
- use descriptive internal links for charts, artists, news, comebacks, live radio, and community pages
- structured data should reflect the visible page purpose where possible

## Writing Tone

K-Beats copy should sound:

- confident
- current
- fan-aware
- concise
- energetic without becoming cheesy

Avoid:

- overlong hype copy
- generic corporate language
- too many exclamation marks
- empty trend language with no content value

## Implementation Rules

When designing or updating pages for K-Beats:

1. start from the homepage's dark-stage composition, not white-card composition
2. use the homepage's approved pink and cyan accents before inventing new colors
3. apply the homepage font pairing: `Cinzel` for headline moments and `Montserrat` for readable UI/body roles
4. keep shapes sharp and layout deliberate
5. verify responsiveness before signoff
6. preserve SEO structure while polishing visuals
